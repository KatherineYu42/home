Warning: this is not a full list. There are many more birds out there, but looking at the amount of typing I have to do for every bird, this list has been limited to more common birds. For a complete list, go to www.allaboutbirds.org or http://toronto-wildlife.com/Birds/birds.html

Red-Winged Blackbird

Appearance: Black, with bold red and yellow markings on wings
Diet: Insects
Habitat: Marsh
Will eat: Mixed grains and seeds (ground feeder)

Black-capped Chickadee

Appearance: Black cap and bib, white cheeks, gray wings and tan chest
Diet: Insects
Habitat: Forest
Will eat: Suet, sunflower and peanuts

House Sparrow

Appearance: White underbelly with black and brown wings and brown cheeks
Diet: Seeds
Habitat: Buildings
Will eat: White millet, corn and sunflower

Mourning Dove

Appearance: Light brown
Diet: Seeds
Habitat: Open Woodland
Will eat: White millet (ground feeder)

Blue Jay


Appearance: Bright blue feathers with white underbelly and crest
Diet: Omnivore
Habitat: Woodland
Will eat: Peanuts, sunflower seeds and suet

Cedar Waxwing

Appearance: Silky tan feathers with striking blue wing tips and tail, red highlight on wing and yellow tail tips
Diet: Fruit
Habitat: Open Woodland
Will eat: native shrubs that bear fruit (eg serviceberry, dogwood)

>

Dark-Eyed Junco

Appearance: Markings are variable, but typically consist of muted grays and browns
Diet: Seeds
Habitat: Forest
Will eat: Sunflower, peanuts, nyjer, millet, corn

Northern Cardinal

Appearance: Striking red feathers with crest and black mask
Diet: Seeds
Habitat: Open Woodland
Will eat: Sunflower
